Neutrinovoltaic Cells

A guide to how we can use them to create electricity.

How does it work?

The Panel


The panel is built with a metamaterial made of graphene and silicon, which is then coated onto a metal-alloy film. The graphene layer consists of layers of ultra-thin sheets of doped graphene, which serves the purpose of amplifiying the kinetic energy of the passing neutrinos.

Collecting Kinetic Energy


When neutrinos pass between these layers, they are not trapped, but they do provide graphene vertical impulses while causing silicon particles to move horizontally. These atomic vibrations create a resonance that is transmitted over to the substrate when the layers are thin enough, and the kinetic energy generated can be turned into electricity.

Converting Kinetic Energy to Electicity


After collecting the kinetic energy, we can convert it to electricity using a cell that converts the optimal level of resonance of the neutrinos into a resonating frequency on an electrical conductor, capturing its energy.
